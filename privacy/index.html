Privacy Policy

Effective Date: March 1, 2026  |  Last Updated: March 20, 2026

LensReport ("we", "our", or "us") operates the LensReport mobile application (the "App"), available on iOS and Android. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, share, and protect your personal information when you use our App.

By downloading, installing, or using LensReport, you agree to the practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the App.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Provide

  • Email address (optional) — collected only if you choose to create an account via email or Google sign-in. You may use the App with anonymous authentication and no personal information.
  • Scanned documents — photographs of receipts, invoices, and other documents that you capture or import through the App for AI-assisted data extraction.

1.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Device identifiers — anonymous identifiers used to associate your device with your account and manage scan quotas.
  • Scan usage counts — the number of scans you perform each billing period, used to enforce plan limits.
  • Crash data — collected via Firebase Crashlytics to diagnose and fix app crashes. Crash reports include device model, OS version, and stack traces. No document content is included in crash reports.
  • Anonymized usage events — collected via Firebase Analytics to understand how features are used and improve the App. These events do not contain document content or personally identifiable information.

1.3 Information We Do Not Collect

  • We do not use Apple's App Tracking Transparency (ATT) framework or any cross-app tracking technologies.
  • We do not include any advertising SDKs or ad networks.
  • We do not perform cross-app or cross-site tracking of any kind.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • AI-powered data extraction — your scanned document images are sent to our cloud infrastructure for processing by artificial intelligence to extract structured data (e.g., vendor names, dates, line items, totals).
  • Account management — to create and maintain your account, manage authentication, and enforce subscription plan limits.
  • Service improvement — anonymized crash data and usage analytics help us identify bugs, improve performance, and prioritize features.
  • Audit trail — we maintain append-only audit logs of actions performed within the App to ensure data integrity and accountability.

3. Third-Party Services and Data Sharing

3.1 Amazon Web Services (AWS)

Document images you scan are uploaded to Amazon Web Services (AWS) cloud storage. AWS processes and stores these images temporarily on our behalf. AWS acts as a data processor under our instructions.

3.2 OpenAI

Document images are sent to OpenAI's API for AI-powered data extraction. OpenAI processes images solely to return structured extraction results to us.

OpenAI Zero Data Retention: We use OpenAI's API with zero data retention enabled. OpenAI does not retain your submitted images or extracted data after processing is complete. OpenAI does not use your data to train, improve, or fine-tune their models.

3.3 Firebase (Google)

We use Firebase for the following services:

  • Firebase Authentication — manages user sign-in. By default, we use anonymous authentication, which does not require any personal information. If you choose to sign up with email or Google, Firebase stores your authentication credentials.
  • Firebase Crashlytics — collects crash reports including device model, OS version, and stack traces to help us diagnose and fix issues. No document content is included.
  • Firebase Analytics — collects anonymized usage events (e.g., feature usage counts, session durations) to help us improve the App. No document content or personally identifiable information is included in analytics events.

3.4 RevenueCat

We use RevenueCat to manage in-app subscriptions and purchases. RevenueCat processes the following data on our behalf:

  • Purchase history — subscription product identifiers, purchase dates, renewal status, and transaction identifiers.
  • Anonymous app user ID — used to associate your subscription state across sessions and devices. No personally identifiable information is shared with RevenueCat unless you have signed in with an email or social account.

RevenueCat acts as a data processor under our instructions and does not use your data for their own marketing or advertising purposes. For more information, see RevenueCat's Privacy Policy.

3.5 No Sale of Data

We do not sell, rent, lease, or trade your personal information to any third party. We do not share your data with third parties for their own marketing purposes.

4. Data Storage and Retention

Data Type Storage Location Retention Period
Scanned document images AWS S3 (cloud) 24 hours — automatically deleted via S3 lifecycle policy
Scan usage counts AWS DynamoDB Retained while your account is active
Account data (email) Firebase Authentication Retained while your account is active
Audit logs Server-side Retained indefinitely, including after account deletion (see Section 4.1)
Local app data (reports, templates, entries) On-device (SQLite) Deleted when you delete your account or uninstall the App
Crash reports Firebase Crashlytics 90 days (per Google's retention policy)
Analytics data Firebase Analytics Retained in anonymized, aggregated form

4.1 Audit Log Retention

LensReport maintains an append-only audit trail that records actions performed within the App (e.g., report creation, entry modifications, exports). Audit logs are retained on our servers even after you delete your account. This is necessary for data integrity, compliance, and fraud prevention. Audit logs do not contain the content of your scanned documents — they record only action types, timestamps, and anonymized identifiers.

5. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal information:

  • All data transmitted between the App and our servers is encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher.
  • Document images are uploaded via short-lived, pre-signed URLs that expire after a single use.
  • Scanned images are automatically and permanently deleted from cloud storage after 24 hours.
  • Local data on your device is stored in an encrypted SQLite database.
  • Firebase Authentication handles credential storage using industry-standard security practices.

No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure. While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your data, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

6. Your Rights and Choices

6.1 Account Deletion

You can delete your account at any time through the Settings screen within the App. Upon account deletion:

  • Your email and authentication credentials are permanently removed from Firebase.
  • Your scan usage data is deleted from our servers.
  • All local data (reports, entries, templates) is deleted from your device.
  • Audit logs are retained as described in Section 4.1.

6.2 Anonymous Use

You may use LensReport with anonymous authentication, which does not require you to provide any personal information such as your name or email address.

6.3 Data Export

You can export your reports as PDF or Excel files at any time through the App's export feature. This allows you to maintain a personal copy of your data.

7. Rights for Users in the European Union (GDPR)

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you have certain rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and related data protection laws:

  • Right of access — You have the right to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification — You have the right to request that we correct any inaccurate or incomplete personal data.
  • Right to erasure — You have the right to request that we delete your personal data, subject to certain legal exceptions (e.g., audit log retention for legitimate interests).
  • Right to data portability — You have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format (e.g., via the App's export features).
  • Right to restrict processing — You have the right to request that we limit the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to object — You have the right to object to the processing of your personal data for certain purposes.
  • Right to withdraw consent — Where we rely on consent as the legal basis for processing, you may withdraw consent at any time.

Legal bases for processing: We process your data based on (a) your consent (e.g., signing up for an account), (b) the performance of a contract (e.g., providing the extraction service), and (c) our legitimate interests (e.g., maintaining audit logs for data integrity and fraud prevention).

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at support@lensreport.app. We will respond within 30 days. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

8. Rights for Users in California (CCPA)

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA) provide you with the following rights:

  • Right to know — You have the right to know what personal information we collect, use, disclose, and sell. This Privacy Policy provides that disclosure.
  • Right to delete — You have the right to request deletion of your personal information, subject to certain exceptions. You can delete your account through the Settings screen in the App, or contact us at support@lensreport.app.
  • Right to opt-out of sale — We do not sell your personal information to third parties. Because we do not sell personal information, there is no need to opt out.
  • Right to non-discrimination — We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of your CCPA rights.
  • Right to correct — You have the right to request correction of inaccurate personal information.

Categories of personal information collected: Identifiers (email address, device identifiers), internet or other electronic network activity information (scan usage counts, anonymized analytics), and visual information (scanned document images, retained for 24 hours only).

Categories of personal information disclosed for a business purpose: Visual information (document images) is disclosed to AWS and OpenAI solely for the purpose of providing the AI extraction service.

To exercise your rights, contact us at support@lensreport.app. We will verify your identity before processing your request.

If you are a parent or guardian and believe that your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us at support@lensreport.app, and we will take steps to delete such information promptly.
